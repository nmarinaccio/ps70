Week 2: 2D Design and Cutting

This week, we focused on 2D/3D Design elements in Fusion as well as how to laser cut specific parts.

3D Modeling

Flathead Screwdriver

I drafted the shaft and tip as a single revolve with a fillet at the neck, then added a parametric handle profile and performed a revolve. Dimensions are driven by a few key parameters so I can easily scale or tweak ergonomics in future iterations.

PVC Joint

The PVC joint started from orthographic sketches. I built the socket and pipe interface with precise diameters and clearances, then created fillets to match injection-molded geometry. The parameters let me generate multiple nominal sizes without remodeling.

Box Building

I designed a simple finger-jointed box and experimented with kerf compensation and tab spacing. The goal was a snug fit that still assembles by hand without splitting. I dialed in offsets and updated the DXF accordingly.

Early joints were too tight; I added kerf offsets and widened tabs slightly to reduce chipping on removal.

The lid alignment uses a shallow rebate that keys into the walls, preventing lateral slop while keeping the lid removable.

I added a bottom support ring for rigidity; this also hides slight imperfections on the base edges after sanding.
