Week 2: 2D Design and Cutting

This week, we focused on 2D/3D Design elements in Fusion as well as how to laser cut specific parts.

3D Modeling

Flathead Screwdriver

Looking around the lab, I wanted to find something that would pose a slight challenge while still doable. This flathead screwdriver fit perfectly. It has interesting ridges, a changing diameter, and an interesting taper to the final screwdriver element at the end. I started by modeling the handel using sketches with arcs. I was able to merge them in the middle with the "Loft" tool to account for the changing diameter. Finally, I used angled planes to create this screwdriver's signature taper look.

PVC Joint

The next interesting looking part that I found was this PVC joint. It acts as a 3-way connector to link multiple PVC pipes. A few key elemets are the intersecting pipe (duhh), the internal changing diameters, and the rounded edges. The tool I learned to use was the filet tool to round the edges.

Box Building

I designed a simple finger-jointed box and experimented with kerf compensation and tab spacing. I also added a few fun features! These include some custom text, a highlighted part window, and some mirrors! The goal was a snug fit that still assembles by hand without splitting. Unfortunately, there was some splitting, but the box came together very well. I am excited to use it as the semester progresses.

I really wanted to have a window into my box. However, clusters of wires, random screws, and other loose parts don't always look that nice. So, I added a window with mirrors to highlight a single part that you can insert into the slot.

For the box window, I decided to use a metal mesh. Not only does it allow a cool view into the box, but it also adds a fun texture for those "texture individuals" out there.

Finally, I added a message for the bottom of my box. As Bobby says, "You can't complete PS70 without all your stuff all over the ground." I hope to forget this message's existance and get a little surprise after spilling my things.
