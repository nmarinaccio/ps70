Week 6: Input Devices

Week 6 was all about input devices: what they are in their most basic form, how to use them, and fun projects with them. We started on Tuesday covering general sensors and then moved to looking at capacitance on Thursday. In addition to building a custom capacitive sensor, we were asked to configure at least one other sensor and include an output device. We also practiced collecting and plotting data, and wrote non-blocking code that avoids delay() in favor of timers or structured logic.

Capacitive Sensor

When thinking about capacitance and capacitors, I always invision a parallel plate capacitor. It is probably because that is the simplest and easiest to teach in an eletricity and magnitism class. However, it was super interesting to realize that almost everything has some level of capacitance and that can be detected. One capcitor that was mentioned on Thursday’s class was the human body. I found this to be super cool, so I decided to make a person detector. I lined two copper plates next to one another but not touching. I then placed my hand specific distances above the sensor to measure the values returned by using the native capacitor code provided to us.

This is the graph produced when mapping the distance above to the output:

It is super interesting to see a substantial dropoff in change. The graph seems almost logorithmic. However, this can work to detect hands for sure!
